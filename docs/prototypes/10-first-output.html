Fear of birth
Feeling terrified about birth doesn't mean something's wrong with you. It means you care and you're paying attention. Most of the fear comes from not knowing what to expect. And that's actually good news — because that part is fixable. You don't need to be fearless. You need to be prepared. There's a difference.

Hospital bag
Your hospital bag doesn't need 47 items from a Pinterest checklist. Here's what actually matters: something comfortable to wear home, snacks you'll actually eat, your phone charger, and one outfit for bub. That's it. Everything else is nice to have. Don't let the packing list stress you out more than the birth.

Asking for help
You're not failing if you need help. You're not weak if you're struggling. You're not a bad mum if you don't love every second of it. You're a person doing something massive for the first time. Of course it's hard. The strongest thing you can do is say "I need support." Not push through alone pretending you're fine.
Post 4 Up next
Partner's role
The number one thing your partner can do during birth isn't hold your hand. It's protect your space. That means: knowing your birth preferences so you don't have to explain them mid-contraction. Asking questions you're too tired to ask. Being the buffer between you and the world. You're doing the work. They're guarding the room.
Post 5 Up next
Trusting yourself
Everyone has an opinion on how you should do this. Your mum, your friends, the internet, that random woman at the shops. Here's what I've learned after years of doing this work: you already know more than you think. You just need the space to hear yourself. That's what good preparation gives you. Not a script. Just confidence that you can handle what comes.
